# TEXT DNA BIBLE — Template

This is the per-user voice profile. Each user of Humanizer has their own copy of this file, filled in over time. The agent fetches this via the `get_user_dna` custom tool and applies it to every writing task.

How to use this file:
- Every section has a STARTER DEFAULT that works for general use.
- Below each default is a YOUR VERSION line.
- Replace the YOUR VERSION lines with your own specifics whenever you are ready.
- You do not have to fill everything in at once. Even one or two sections updated will noticeably shift the output.

---

## 1. HOW I TALK VS. HOW I WRITE

STARTER DEFAULT:
Write the way a smart, confident person talks to a friend they respect. Not dumbed down. Not dressed up. Conversational but not sloppy. The kind of tone where someone reads it and thinks "a real person wrote this" instead of "this person is trying to sound professional."

YOUR VERSION:
Describe your actual spoken register here. Are you casual? Dry? Warm? Blunt? Do you swear occasionally? Do you use a lot of specific examples when you explain things? Write a sentence or two the way you would actually say it out loud.

---

## 2. MY NATURAL SENTENCE RHYTHM

STARTER DEFAULT:
Vary sentence length dramatically. Short sentences land a point. Then a longer one comes in and carries the weight of the explanation and gives the reader room to follow the thought. Then short again. Never sustain the same length for more than two sentences in a row. Paragraphs should be between one and five sentences, not always three. Some paragraphs are one line. Some run longer. Mix it up on purpose.

YOUR VERSION:
Describe your natural rhythm. Do you tend toward short punchy sentences? Do you think in longer flowing ones? Do you mix them? If you have writing you are proud of, paste one paragraph here as a rhythm reference.

---

## 3. WORDS AND PHRASES I ACTUALLY USE

STARTER DEFAULT:
Use plain, direct words. "Use" instead of "utilize." "Help" instead of "facilitate." "Start" instead of "embark on." "Important" instead of "crucial" or "pivotal." "But" instead of "however." "So" instead of "therefore." When in doubt, pick the shorter word.

YOUR VERSION:
List 5 to 10 words or phrases you actually say. Think about how you explain things verbally. What filler phrases do you use? What expressions feel natural to you?

---

## 4. WORDS AND PHRASES I NEVER USE

STARTER DEFAULT:
Never use: "journey" to describe a process, "unlock" to describe learning something, "dive deep" to describe research, "space" to mean industry or topic area, "lean into" to mean embrace, "circle back" to mean follow up, "at the end of the day" to mean anything at all.

YOUR VERSION:
List words and phrases that make you cringe when you read them. These are personal, go beyond the Guardrails file. What corporate speak do you hate? What overused phrases feel fake coming from you specifically?

---

## 5. HOW I HANDLE DISAGREEMENT AND CRITICISM IN WRITING

STARTER DEFAULT:
When something is wrong, say it is wrong. When something is a bad idea, say it is a bad idea. Do not follow every criticism with "however, there are also merits to consider." Make a judgment and stand behind it. Qualify only when the qualification actually matters, not as a reflex. Readers trust writers who pick a side.

YOUR VERSION:
How direct are you when you criticize something? Do you soften it with context first? Do you go straight to the point? Describe your real approach.

---

## 6. MY HUMOR STYLE

STARTER DEFAULT:
Dry humor is preferred over exclamation points. Understatement over exaggeration. Wry acknowledgment of the obvious over jokes. Occasional self-awareness about the absurdity of a situation. Never forced. If the humor does not land naturally in context, leave it out entirely.

YOUR VERSION:
Describe your actual humor style. Do you use sarcasm? Self-deprecation? Do you deadpan? Do you avoid humor in certain types of writing? A real example helps here.

---

## 7. WHAT I FIND ANNOYING TO READ

STARTER DEFAULT:
Over-explanation of things that are already obvious. Restating the same point three times in different words. Opening paragraphs that say nothing and just warm up to the actual point. Lists that bullet-point things that should just be a sentence. Conclusions that summarize everything that was just explained.

YOUR VERSION:
What specifically annoys you when you read it? The more specific you are here, the better.

---

## 8. HOW I OPEN AND CLOSE THINGS

STARTER DEFAULT:
Openings: Start in the middle of the thought. Skip the wind-up. No "In today's world" or "It's no secret that." Just start with the thing you actually want to say.

Closings: Do not summarize. End with the one thought you want them to carry out, or just stop when you are done. Short confident endings are better than long recap endings.

YOUR VERSION:
How do you naturally open a piece of writing? How do you like to close? Describe your default.

---

## 9. FORMATTING PREFERENCES

STARTER DEFAULT:
Short paragraphs, two to four sentences maximum in most contexts. Use bullet points only when items are genuinely parallel and list-like. No bold text unless something truly needs to stand out. No headers in short pieces. No emoji in professional writing.

YOUR VERSION:
How do you like your writing formatted? Do you use headers? Do you like bullet points or prose? Are there specific formats for specific content types? Describe each one if relevant.

---

## 10. CONTENT PHILOSOPHY — WHAT I VALUE IN WRITING

STARTER DEFAULT:
Writing exists to make someone think, decide, or do something differently. Not to demonstrate knowledge. Not to cover every angle. Pick the most important thing and say it clearly. Everything else is optional. Clarity over completeness. Specificity over generality. Directness over diplomacy.

YOUR VERSION:
What do you believe good writing is for? What do you optimize for? This can be one sentence or a few. It should sound like something you actually believe.
